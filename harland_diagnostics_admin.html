AP Science Readiness Diagnostic
This assessment maps your current knowledge across Physics, Chemistry, and Biology — the three AP science options — so your Head Teacher can recommend which one to start with and how long the preparation pathway will be.

The questions cover all three subjects at different levels of difficulty. Some will feel easy, some hard, some completely unfamiliar. All of that is useful information. Answer as honestly as you can — "I don't know" tells us just as much as a correct answer.
~45–50 minutes 5 domains Physics · Chemistry · Biology No notes or calculator
Domain A · Physics
Physics — Concepts & Calculation
Covers G8 foundations through G9–12 high school physics.
A1
A rocket in deep space fires its engines briefly then shuts them off. What happens to the rocket's motion afterwards?
A2
You push a heavy box across the floor at constant speed. A friend says "there must be a net force since it's moving." Are they right?
A3
Using F = ma, calculate the net force on a 6 kg object that accelerates at 5 m/s². Show your working.

Extension (attempt if you can): A 50 kg person stands on a scale in a lift accelerating upward at 2 m/s² (g = 10 m/s²). What does the scale read, and why is it different from their normal weight?
A4
A ball thrown upward reaches its highest point. At that exact moment, which is correct?
A5
In your own words, explain what a velocity-time graph tells you. What does the slope represent? What does the area under the line represent?
A6
How comfortable do you feel with physics maths — setting up and solving equations?
Very uncomfortableVery comfortable
Domain B · Chemistry
Chemistry — Concepts & Reasoning
Covers topics from your AEI Physical Science course through G9–12 chemistry concepts.
B1
What is the difference between an element, a compound, and a mixture? Give a real-world example of each.
B2
When iron rusts, it reacts with oxygen to form iron oxide. The total mass of iron + oxygen before the reaction equals the mass of iron oxide after. What law does this illustrate, and what does it tell us about atoms during the reaction?
B3
Without using a textbook definition, explain in your own words what an acid does and what a base does in a solution. Give one real-world example of each.
B4
An atom of carbon has 6 protons, 6 neutrons, and 6 electrons. If you remove one electron, what happens to the atom's charge? What is this particle now called?
B5
Balance this chemical equation: H₂ + O₂ → H₂O. Write the balanced equation and explain in one sentence why balancing is necessary.
B6
How comfortable do you feel with chemistry concepts — understanding how and why substances behave the way they do?
Very uncomfortableVery comfortable
Domain C · Biology
Biology — Concepts & Systems Thinking
You haven't formally studied biology at AEI — answer based on what you already know from any source.
C1
What is the main difference between a plant cell and an animal cell? Name one structure found in plant cells that is not found in animal cells, and explain what it does.
C2
During photosynthesis, plants convert light energy into stored chemical energy. What are the two main inputs to this process, and what is the main output?
C3
DNA carries genetic information. In your own words, explain how the information in DNA leads to the production of a protein. You don't need exact terminology — explain the process as best you can.
C4
In an ecosystem, energy flows from producers to consumers. A rabbit eats grass, and a fox eats the rabbit. If the grass contains 10,000 units of energy, approximately how much energy does the fox receive, assuming 10% efficiency at each level?
C5
How comfortable do you feel with biology — remembering terms, understanding how living systems work?
Very uncomfortableVery comfortable
Domain D · Cross-Science
Cross-Science Reasoning
Questions that blend subjects or test how you apply scientific thinking generally.
D1
A scientist measures the speed of a chemical reaction at different temperatures and finds it doubles for every 10°C increase. This involves both chemistry and physics concepts. Explain why temperature affects reaction rate using what you know about particles and energy.
D2
When you exercise hard, your heart beats faster and you breathe more quickly. Using what you know about both biology and chemistry, explain why this happens. What is your body trying to do, and what chemical changes are driving it?
D3
Which of these descriptions best matches how you think about science problems?
